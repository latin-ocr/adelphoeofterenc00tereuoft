Sc. 3.

Demea has heard, during his delay in the city, that Ctesipho, his
son, has been mixed up in the abduction of the music-girl.
Seeking Ctesipho at his brother's house, he finds Syrus, who
assures him that Ctesipho rebuked Aeschinus for his evil deeds,
and then went home. Demea expresses his joy in the conduct
of Ctesipho and tells how he was educated.
